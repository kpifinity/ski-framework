## LinkedIn DRAFT — SKI Framework showcase page (LP4)

SKI produces exactly five verdict types. No confidence scores, no probability ranges — because "87% compliant" is not something you can defend.

CLEAR — applicable rules evaluated, no issue detected.

NULL_UNMAPPED — the subject isn't in the knowledge graph yet (an honest "we don't cover this").

The point of a small, binary-ish taxonomy is reconstructability. Every verdict carries its provenance — which knowledge-graph version, which citations, which checks — so an auditor can re-derive it. A score can't be re-derived. A verdict can.
